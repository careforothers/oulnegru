The social reality is performative. Social phenomenons appear because we affirm them and we organize ourselves to act in the direction of their apparition. They are not like mountains, volcanoes, oceans, birds, that exist independently of our words, actions, desires. Social phenomenons are more similar to collective dreams. The human rights, for instance, grew from a performative input of a tiny group of dreamers that shared the same social dream. It took centuries and it is still far from being completely realized, but a tiny group of people changed the social reality by adding to it "human rights". We live in a world of multiple crises, so, more than ever, we need to remember that simple truth and to find ways to master and to learn our children to master this performative aspect of the social reality.
 
Adriana Chiruta

initiator and curator of

Organic* cooperation is something that can be observed in nature, but also in groups of people, during trainings based on dance, theater or music improvisation techniques. When organic cooperation occurs, it offers the chance of one of the best aesthetic experiences (in the `relational aesthetics` realm (Bourriaud,1990)). Contemporary Artists` Warning to Humanity

Three years ago, in 2017, 15364 scientists from 184 countries signed “Warning to Humanity: A Second Notice”. This document came as a continuation of the first notice “World Scientists’ Warning to Humanity”, penned in 1992 by the Union of Concerned Scientists with more than 1700 independent scientists, including the majority of Nobel laureates of that time. These two moments probably marked the largest gatherings of brainpower ever to happen on the face of Earth. Both warnings cautioned that:
“a great change in our stewardship of the Earth and the life on it is required, if vast human misery is to be avoided.”
"Stewardship of the Earth" is used in the sense of taking care of, of caring for, of doing the necessary things for someone who needs help or protection, of treating something in such a way that it stays in good condition. In one word of loving. The largest gathering of brainpower ever to happen on the face of Earth reached this simple conclusion:
that the distance between humanity and its vast misery can be accurately measured by our capacity of loving.
As simple and as elegant as that.

Other Performance Species 2021

The majority of the performing arts sector and contemporary art world is wondering now how shall "our normality" be preserved under the new terms and rules imposed to humanity by COVID-19 pandemics. But what if it cannot be preserved? What if the only way to continue to work is by inventing "Other Performance Species" (abbreviated OPS), adapted even to situations of worst futures set-ups? Embracing the new cooperation paradigm in life sciences (F. de Waal, Lynn Mangulis), as well as the performative turn in cognitive and social sciences (Gerald Edelman, John R. Searle), our performative research group will question the premises of the so called "normality“ in performing arts, its main concepts, paradigms and political frames in order to reframe, change and enlarge the ways in which performing arts sector can still work and be relevant in nowadays world.

and the CARE FOR OTHERS performative research nucleus is:

Adriana Chiruta

is a hybrid artist: theatre director passionate about laboratory theatres and postmodern dance, post-conceptual artist and
philosopher (with interests in logic and in political philosophy. Since 2014, she's been building, together with her very loved one, a writer, an invented country for independent artists and researchers, an ecolaboratory of contemporary art called Carambach. Beside this main life-art project, she inquires the possibility of devising radically participatory performing art forms that may allow her and others to work directly with the public, without the actors/dancers/performers` intercession. Other Performance Species concept emerge out of that research.

Anastasia Osoianu

With a background in psychology and neuroscience (TU Dresden, Max Plank Institut & CRI Paris), Anastasia Osoianu (alias Meer) creates poetry, visual and cinematic artworks at the juncture of disciplines, media, polarities of gender and cultures, intimacy and publicity.
Since 2017 she is performing poetry on festivals and spoken word stages in Europe, collaborating with visual artists in/from Moldova and internationally and exhibiting her works at various cultural spots, where she often uses participatory performative and interactive frameworks to facilitate a reflective dialogue across participants, inter-action and
empathy.

Laura Trocan

Performing arts professional and sociologist, Laura Trocan is an independent producer based in Bucharest. With a BA in Sociology and an MA in cultural studies and performing arts, she works in the performing arts field since 2016, producing and coordinating contemporary dance performances, theater and interdisciplinary projects. Laura is interested in how a performance reflects and questions the society's social constructs and the performative side of social roles. She is also interested in documentary theater as a way of involving communities in the creative process and also to reflect as accurate as possible the real social dynamics, in order to question them. For the time being, Laura wonders how (and actually if) we can create live performances in the absence of liveness.
